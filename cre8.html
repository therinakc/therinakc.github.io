For UX Laurier‚Äôs designathon, teams were tasked with creating a solution to help encourage users rekindle their curiosity and inner child to enhance their learning. My partner and I designed Cre8, a mobile app that provides a way to get in touch with your inner child by seeing art in everyday life and drawing anything you see in your surroundings!

With Cre8, users get a random daily notification to stop and take a picture wherever they are, and draw on their phone anything they see, from a turtle to a smiley face :)

Problem

Have you ever watched a child explore their environment with wonder and curiosity? They're like little scientists, always testing and experimenting, eager to learn about the world around them. As adults, we often lose touch with that sense of wonder and curiosity. But when it comes to children, fostering that sense of exploration is essential for their growth and development.

As adults, it's also important for us to reconnect with that sense of wonder and curiosity we had as children. We can explore unknown territories, find comfort in unfamiliar places, build new relationships, discover hidden passions and talents, and so much more.

By intentionally tapping into our inner child, we'll keep our minds alive with wonder and open to a world of endless opportunities.

About the Project

From this, teams were given the problem of:

How might we design an experience that sparks curiosity, self-reflection, and experimentation to enhance learning and discovery?

The challenge of figuring out our solution was that we had to consider learning/creativity and exploration in various aspects of life. This meant that we were open to tackle any aspect that we felt is not addressed enough in users‚Äô everyday lives.

Our ideas ranged from helping users learn a new skill such as a musical instrument or language, to exploring art and user's creative side. One thing that my partner and I wanted to ensure our solution had is an app that can be used during one's busy schedule to give them a break.

Takeaways & What I Learned

This designathon was an amazing learning opportunity for me! With all the sponsors and workshops that the event had, I was able to get the most out of them and get a break from designing our solution by learning from industry professionals.

I not only learned a lot from the workshops I attended, but also from the whole UX process within the tight timeline as well as seeing other people‚Äôs solutions and seeing where we can improve!

Getting feedback from professionals in the field helped my partner and I to see where we can focus our solution without including elements that overwhelm users. The problem given to us helped me to also rediscover my own creativity and have fun in designing a solution while learning at the same time. :)

Next Steps

  • Emphasizing feasibility: One of the commnets we got was that we can go deeper into our solution and ensure feasibility even further.
  • Increasing creativity: Another comment we got is that AI might hinder user's creativity. It would be interesting to consider taking this out so users can take more time to draw.
