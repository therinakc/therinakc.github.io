In Winter 2022, I interned at the Financial Accountability Office of Ontario as a Digital Graphics Co-op Student as part of the Communications team.

What is the FAO?

The Financial Accountability Office of Ontario or FAO is a provincial organization that provides independent analysis on the state of the Province's finances, trends in the provincial economy and related matters important to the Legislative Assembly of Ontario.

What I did

Working closely with the lead graphic designer, I was assigned various digital communications tasks and products to complete for publication.

Infographics

Utilizing Adobe CC (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro), I designed infographics based on reports written by the Financial and Economics teams, to be published on social media.

Formatting Reports

Formatting reports using Microsoft Office and Adobe Acrobat, then converting them to HTML and CSS, ensuring that they were accessible and user friendly to read.

Website Maintenance

Maintaining the FAO website using HTML, CSS, and a CMS interface.

Accessibility

All FAO digital communications products have to meet with current government standards. Things such as, Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) requirements and guidelines, French Language Services Act (FLSA), and the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (FIPPA) have to be met in order for products to be approved and released.

Some of the work I did!

  • Infographics were designed to be posted on different social media such as Instagram and Twitter.
  • Each infographic had to be created in English...
  • ...and French.
