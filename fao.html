In Winter 2022, I interned at the Financial Accountability Office of Ontario as a Digital Graphics Co-op Student as part of the Communications team.

What is the FAO?

The Financial Accountability Office of Ontario or FAO is a provincial organization that provides independent analysis on the state of the Province's finances, trends in the provincial economy and related matters important to the Legislative Assembly of Ontario.

What I did

I worked with the Communication teams, which is an amazing team of creative individuals who constantly supported each other and provided meaningful feedback on products. I mainly worked closely with the lead graphic designer and I was assigned various digital communications tasks and products to complete for publication. Typically if a report had to be released, each of these tasks were part of a process done within a week's timeilne

Infographics

Utilizing Adobe CC (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro), I designed infographics based on reports written by the Financial and Economics teams, to be published on social media.

  • Infographics were designed to be posted on social media such as Instagram and Twitter.
  • Each infographic had to be created in English...
  • ...and French.

Formatting Reports

Reports to be published were formatted using Microsoft Office and Adobe Acrobat (for PDFs), then converted to HTML and CSS for the website, ensuring that they were accessible and user friendly to read.

Website Maintenance

I assisted in maintaining the FAO website using HTML, CSS, and a CMS interface. This also involved me taking lead in preparing communications for public release.

Ensuring Accessibility in Products

All FAO digital communications products have to meet with current government standards. Things such as, Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) requirements and guidelines, French Language Services Act (FLSA), and the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (FIPPA) have to be met in order for products to be approved and released.
This included consideration for colour choices, font sizes and readability, and creating graphical descriptions for tables & graphs.
